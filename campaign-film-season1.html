Campaign Overview

Tamil Nadu's first alcohol awareness film contest

Launched in January 2025, the "Say No to Alcohol" Short Film Contest called on students, educators, and creative minds across Tamil Nadu to use the power of cinema to shift attitudes about alcohol and substance abuse.

The response exceeded all expectations. Within the first two weeks of launch, over 150 registrations poured in from colleges, schools, and independent filmmakers — many of them with personal connections to the cause they were portraying.

Films arrived in Tamil, English, and silent formats — spanning documentary, narrative, animation, and experimental genres. A panel of five judges drawn from Tamil cinema, public health, and social work reviewed all 312 valid entries over three weeks.

The winning films were subsequently screened at 23 schools and colleges across Tamil Nadu as part of our ongoing "Short Films on Wheels" initiative, reaching an estimated audience of 12,000+ students.

"We did not expect 312 films. We expected 50. What we received instead was a generation's voice — clear, powerful, and refusing to be silenced."
Grace Foundation Campaign Director
