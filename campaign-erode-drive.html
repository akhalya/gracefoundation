Campaign Overview

Grace Foundation's first grassroots school drive

Launched in October 2024, the Erode District School Awareness Drive was Grace Foundation's first large-scale grassroots outreach program — and the campaign that established our model for school awareness across Tamil Nadu.

Erode was chosen deliberately. The district has above-average reported rates of alcohol dependency, a high proportion of young men in unorganised labour, and limited access to mental health resources. It was exactly the community that needed this conversation most.

Over three months, our team of facilitators visited all 18 participating government schools — conducting two-hour interactive sessions with students from Class 8 through Class 12. Sessions covered facts about alcohol and its effects on brain development, strategies for handling peer pressure, and how to recognise and respond to addiction in a family member.

Parallel parent workshops ran at 12 schools on weekend evenings — a critical component, since parental awareness and response has a direct bearing on adolescent drinking behavior.

The campaign closed with Youth Pledge Ceremonies at each school, where students signed individual pledges to remain substance-free — witnessed by teachers, parents, and local panchayat leaders.

"At one school in Bhavani, a Class 10 student approached us after the session and said she finally understood what her father was going through. We stayed an extra two hours that day. That is what this work is."

Program Design

How each school session worked

1

Icebreaker & Trust Building (20 min)

Facilitators opened with non-threatening activities to build rapport and create a safe space before the topic was introduced.

2

The Reality of Alcohol (30 min)

Age-appropriate presentation on how alcohol affects the brain, body, academic performance, and family — with real data from Tamil Nadu.

3

Peer Pressure Roleplay (25 min)

Students acted out scenarios involving peer pressure, practising refusal skills in a safe, facilitated environment.

4

Family & Community (20 min)

Discussion on how to support a family member struggling with dependency — without shame, and with practical steps.

5

Q&A and Anonymous Questions (15 min)

Students submitted anonymous questions on paper. Every question was answered openly — some sessions ran 30+ minutes over time.

6

Youth Pledge Ceremony (10 min)

Students signed individual pledge cards. These were collected, framed, and gifted back to each school to display in their common areas.
